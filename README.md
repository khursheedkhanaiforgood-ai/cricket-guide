# A Beginner's Guide to Cricket 🏏

## Introduction

Welcome to cricket! Whether you're curious about this ancient sport or want to understand what's happening when you watch a match, you're in the right place. Cricket is one of the **oldest team sports in the world**, with a rich history and exciting modern formats. Let's dive in!

---

## History of Cricket

### The First Cricket Match

The first **recorded** cricket match was played in **1646** in Kent, England. However, cricket almost certainly existed before that — the 1646 record is simply the earliest we have written evidence for.

### How It Began

- Cricket is believed to have begun as a **children's game** in the villages of south-east England (Kent, Sussex, Surrey) sometime in the **late 1500s**
- By the **early 1700s**, adults were playing it seriously, and people were even **betting large amounts of money** on matches
- The first match between **two county teams** that we know about was **Kent vs Surrey in 1709**
- The famous **Marylebone Cricket Club (MCC)** — which wrote the official rules — was founded in **1787**
- **Lord's Cricket Ground** in London, the MCC's home ground, is still called *"the home of cricket"* today

## Glossary

- **Batting**: The act of hitting the ball with a bat to score runs
- **Bowling**: Throwing the ball towards the batter (called a "bowler")
- **County Teams**: Regional teams that compete in domestic cricket
- **Marylebone Cricket Club (MCC)**: The organization that established cricket's official rules
- **Recorded Match**: A match that has written historical documentation
- **Runs**: Points scored in cricket
